📖 What These Terms Mean (Plain English)

P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)

How many shillings you pay for every 1 shilling of profit. Low P/E (<15) = potentially cheap. High P/E (>25) = investors expect fast growth. {% if fundamentals.pe_ratio %}
{{ interpret_pe(fundamentals.pe_ratio) }} {% endif %}

PEG Ratio (Price/Earnings to Growth)

P/E divided by earnings growth rate. PEG < 1.0 may mean the stock is undervalued relative to its growth. {% if fundamentals.peg_ratio %}
{{ interpret_peg(fundamentals.peg_ratio) }} {% endif %}

ROE (Return on Equity)

How much profit the company makes with shareholder money. 15%+ = good, 20%+ = excellent. {% if fundamentals.roe %}
{{ interpret_roe(fundamentals.roe) }} {% endif %}

ROIC (Return on Invested Capital)

How well the company uses ALL invested money (from shareholders AND lenders) to generate profit. ROIC > 15% is strong.

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Momentum indicator from 0-100. Above 70 = overbought (price ran up fast, may pull back). Below 30 = oversold (price fell too far, may bounce). 30-70 = neutral. {% if latest.rsi %}
{{ interpret_rsi(latest.rsi) }} {% endif %}

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

Tracks the relationship between two moving averages. MACD above signal line = bullish. MACD below signal line = bearish. A "cross" signals a potential trend change.

Bollinger Bands

Price 'envelope' around a moving average. Price near upper band = relatively expensive. Price near lower band = relatively cheap. Bands widen when volatility increases.

Debt-to-Equity

How much debt vs. shareholder equity. Below 0.5 = conservative (low risk). Above 2.0 = aggressive (high risk). {% if fundamentals.debt_to_equity %}
{{ interpret_de(fundamentals.debt_to_equity) }} {% endif %}

Operating Margin

Percentage of revenue left after paying for operations. Higher = more efficient business. 20%+ is generally strong.

Net Margin

Percentage of revenue that becomes actual profit after ALL costs. The 'bottom line' — higher is better.

EPS Growth

How fast earnings per share are growing year-over-year. 10%+ is solid. 20%+ is excellent. Negative = profits shrinking.

Revenue Growth

How fast total sales are growing year-over-year. Growing revenue = expanding business. Falling = may be losing market share.
